SOURCE: Derbyshire Medicines Management NHS Guidelines / BCSH Guidelines (Keeling et al.) / BNF 2024
SEVERITY: REFERENCE DOCUMENT
CATEGORY: Clinical Management

WARFARIN — INDICATIONS AND TARGET INR
Atrial Fibrillation: Target INR 2.5 (range 2.0-3.0)
DVT/PE treatment: Target INR 2.5 (range 2.0-3.0)
Mechanical heart valves (low risk): Target INR 2.5-3.0
Mechanical heart valves (high risk): Target INR 3.0-3.5
Antiphospholipid syndrome: Target INR 2.5-3.5

STARTING WARFARIN
- Slow loading regimen (primary care, AF, no urgent anticoagulation needed):
  Day 1-2: Warfarin 2mg daily
  Check INR at Day 14 and adjust dose accordingly
  Target: INR 2-3 within 1-2 weeks
- Do NOT use loading doses unless supervised — incorrect loading can cause severe haemorrhage or sub-therapeutic anticoagulation

ANTIPLATELET THERAPY AND WARFARIN
IMPORTANT: When starting warfarin, antiplatelet therapy (aspirin, clopidogrel, dipyridamole, ticagrelor, prasugrel) should be STOPPED once INR is within therapeutic range, UNLESS continuation is explicitly advised by a consultant in secondary care. This must be documented.
Exceptions where dual therapy is appropriate:
- Mechanical heart valve + recent ACS (specialist decision)
- Post-PCI in AF (specialist decision, limited duration)
- High thrombotic risk (must be individually assessed)

DRUG INTERACTIONS WITH WARFARIN
Drugs that INCREASE INR (inhibit warfarin metabolism or have additive anticoagulant effects):
MAJOR interactions (avoid or intensive monitoring):
- Metronidazole/tinidazole: Inhibit CYP2C9 — INR increases within 24-48 hours, can double INR
- Fluconazole/miconazole: Potent CYP2C9/CYP3A4 inhibition — dramatic INR increases
- Amiodarone: Inhibits CYP2C9 and CYP3A4 — gradual but sustained INR increase; warfarin dose reduction of 30-50% required
- Ciprofloxacin/metronidazole: INR increases, mechanism not fully established, monitor closely
- Aspirin (high dose): Additive antihaemostatic effect + COX inhibition
- Trimethoprim/co-trimoxazole: Inhibit CYP2C9 — INR increases within days
Monitoring recommendation: Check INR within 72 hours of starting any of the above, and again within 5-7 days

Drugs that DECREASE INR (induce warfarin metabolism):
- Rifampicin: Potent CYP inducer — can reduce INR dramatically within days
- Carbamazepine, phenytoin, phenobarbital: CYP inducers — reduce warfarin effect
- St John's Wort: CYP3A4/2C9 inducer — commonly missed (herbal, not considered 'medication' by patients)
- Monitoring: Check INR weekly when enzyme inducer started or stopped

BLEEDING MANAGEMENT
Minor bleeding (epistaxis, bruising): Check INR, reduce dose if supratherapeutic
INR 5-8, no bleeding: Withhold 1-2 doses, recheck INR
INR >8, no bleeding or minor bleeding: Stop warfarin, give Vitamin K 1-5mg oral, recheck in 24h
Major bleeding (any site): Stop warfarin, IV Vitamin K 5-10mg, Prothrombin Complex Concentrate (PCC), urgent haematology

PATIENT COUNSELLING KEY POINTS
1. Keep a consistent diet — large changes in Vitamin K intake (green leafy vegetables) affect INR
2. Always carry anticoagulant alert card
3. Tell every prescriber, pharmacist, and dentist you are on warfarin
4. Report any unusual bleeding or bruising immediately
5. Avoid OTC ibuprofen/aspirin without medical advice
6. Alcohol: moderate intake is acceptable; binge drinking increases bleeding risk
7. If you miss a dose: take it if remembered same day, skip if next day — never double dose

DISCLAIMER: This document is for clinical reference only. Drug interaction management must be individualised by a qualified clinician or pharmacist.
