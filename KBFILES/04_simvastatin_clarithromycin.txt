DOCUMENT: Simvastatin + Clarithromycin Drug Interaction — Rhabdomyolysis Risk
SOURCE: FDA Zocor Label / MHRA SPS Guidelines / Oxford Medical Case Reports 2018 / DailyMed NLM
SEVERITY: MAJOR — CONTRAINDICATED
DRUG_PAIR: simvastatin + clarithromycin

INTERACTION SUMMARY
Simvastatin + clarithromycin is a CONTRAINDICATED drug combination according to both FDA prescribing information and MHRA/SPS UK guidelines. Clarithromycin is a potent inhibitor of CYP3A4, the primary enzyme responsible for simvastatin metabolism. Co-administration leads to dramatically increased simvastatin plasma levels and carries a serious risk of rhabdomyolysis — potentially leading to acute kidney injury, hyperkalaemia, and death. Simvastatin must be SUSPENDED for the entire duration of clarithromycin treatment.

MECHANISM
Simvastatin (a CYP3A4 substrate) is predominantly metabolised by CYP3A4 in the liver. Clarithromycin is one of the most potent CYP3A4 inhibitors available:
- Clarithromycin inhibits CYP3A4 AND inhibits OATP1B1/1B3 hepatic uptake transporters
- Combined enzyme + transporter inhibition markedly reduces simvastatin first-pass metabolism
- Simvastatin AUC increases by approximately 8-17 fold with strong CYP3A4 inhibitors
- Elevated simvastatin acid levels cause dose-dependent skeletal muscle toxicity
- Mechanism of myotoxicity: mitochondrial dysfunction in muscle cells, impaired CoQ10 synthesis, disrupted membrane integrity → myocyte death → rhabdomyolysis

RHABDOMYOLYSIS CLINICAL FEATURES
- Muscle pain, weakness, tenderness (proximal muscles first)
- Dark cola-coloured urine (myoglobinuria)
- Markedly elevated creatine kinase (CK) — typically >10x ULN (>2,500 U/L); severe cases >100,000 U/L
- Acute kidney injury: creatinine rises within 24-72 hours
- Hyperkalaemia: released from damaged myocytes, can cause fatal arrhythmia
- Case report (Oxford Medical Case Reports 2018): CK peaked at 223,859 U/L, creatinine rose from 63 to 325 μmol/L, severe hyperkalaemia (7.4 mmol/L) requiring ICU-level management

CLINICAL EVIDENCE
- FDA Zocor prescribing information: "Combination of [clarithromycin] with simvastatin is CONTRAINDICATED. If treatment with clarithromycin is unavoidable, therapy with simvastatin MUST be suspended during the course of treatment."
- FDA adverse event analysis (2002): Over 50% of statin-induced rhabdomyolysis cases were due to drug interactions.
- UK Primary care study (2012): 30% of patients were prescribed a CYP3A4 inhibitor concurrently with a CYP3A4-metabolised statin over a 1-year period.
- MHRA/SPS Guidelines (2023): Clarithromycin is CONTRAINDICATED with simvastatin. If clarithromycin is essential, simvastatin MUST be stopped whilst taking the antibiotic course.
- Rhabdomyolysis risk with simvastatin + CYP3A4 inhibitor is approximately 18.7-fold higher than simvastatin alone.

DOSE CONSIDERATIONS
- Dose-related risk: Simvastatin 80mg daily has a myopathy incidence of 0.9% vs 0.02% at 20mg/day in clinical trials WITHOUT CYP3A4 inhibitors — the combination with clarithromycin amplifies this massively
- Even low-dose simvastatin (20mg) carries significant rhabdomyolysis risk with clarithromycin
- There is NO safe dose combination — the interaction is pharmacologically fundamental, not dose-dependent

CLINICAL ACTION
IMMEDIATE ACTION REQUIRED:
1. STOP simvastatin immediately when clarithromycin is prescribed
2. Do not restart simvastatin until clarithromycin course is fully complete
3. If patient has any muscle symptoms (pain, weakness, dark urine): check CK urgently, refer to emergency department
4. Duration of simvastatin suspension: entire course of clarithromycin plus 2-3 days after completion (clarithromycin half-life ~5 hours, but metabolites persist)

ANTIBIOTIC ALTERNATIVES (if infection requires macrolide):
- Azithromycin: Does NOT significantly inhibit CYP3A4 — simvastatin can usually be continued (monitor, rare interaction reports)
- Amoxicillin/co-amoxiclav: No CYP3A4 interaction with statins

STATIN ALTERNATIVES (if clarithromycin course is unavoidable and statin cannot be stopped):
- Rosuvastatin: Minimal CYP3A4 metabolism — significantly lower interaction risk, monitor closely
- Fluvastatin: CYP2C9 substrate predominantly, can be continued with clarithromycin with caution
- Pravastatin: Minimal CYP metabolism — can be continued with clarithromycin
NOT acceptable alternatives: Atorvastatin (CYP3A4 substrate — avoid with clarithromycin), Lovastatin (CYP3A4 substrate — contraindicated)

MONITORING IF COMBINATION CANNOT BE AVOIDED
- Baseline CK before starting clarithromycin
- CK on day 3 and day 7 of clarithromycin course
- Creatinine and electrolytes if CK rising
- Immediate cessation of both drugs if CK >5x ULN or symptoms develop

DISCLAIMER: This document is for clinical reference only. Drug interaction management must be individualised by a qualified clinician or pharmacist.
